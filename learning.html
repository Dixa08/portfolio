Lab 5: Communication Parameters

  • Grooming: Hair, makeup, accessories, wristwatch, formal footwear, and attire contribute to a professional appearance.
  • Verbal Communication: Tone, pitch, volume, pauses, voice modulation, and articulation help in delivering messages effectively.
  • Non-Verbal Communication: Eye contact, smile, gait (walking style), hand gestures, facial expressions, posture, and body language reflect confidence and clarity.
  • Content & Knowledge: Strong research, clear concepts, and use of ethos (credibility), pathos (emotion), and logos (logic). Also includes answering key “WH” questions (what, why, how, when).
  • X-Factor: Confidence, personality, stage presence, and overall persona that make communication impactful and memorable.
  • Mehrabian Model of Communication: Suggests that communication is 7% words, 38% tone of voice, and 55% body language, highlighting the importance of non-verbal cues.
  • Theory of Instant Gratification: Refers to the desire for immediate results or rewards, which can affect communication style and patience in conversations.
  • Concept of Ikigai: A Japanese concept meaning "reason for being." It helps individuals align passion, mission, profession, and vocation for meaningful communication and life goals.
